## Company Overview
**1. Business Overview**  
Adobe Inc. develops and markets creative, document, and digital experience software used to create, manage, and optimize digital content and customer experiences. Core offerings include Creative Cloud (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere), Document Cloud (Acrobat), and Experience Cloud for marketing automation and analytics. The company operates a subscription-first model with the vast majority of revenue derived from recurring Digital Media and Digital Experience ARR.

**1. Results summary**  
Revenue of $6.62B beat consensus (~$6.45B) with 13% YoY growth. Non-GAAP EPS of $5.96 beat estimates by 0.4%. Subscription revenue reached $6.39B (+14% YoY), led by Business Professionals & Consumers (+16%) and Creative & Marketing Professionals (+13%). AI-first ARR more than tripled YoY and exceeded $500M. Total ARR hit $27.10B.

**2. Guidance analysis**  
Company raised full-year FY26 revenue to $26.50–26.60B and non-GAAP EPS to $24.35–24.45 (both above prior expectations). Q3 revenue guided at $6.67–6.72B with non-GAAP EPS of $6.05–6.10. Guidance incorporates Semrush acquisition and assumes current macro conditions; prior company guidance was exceeded on both top and bottom line.

**3. Earnings quality**  
GAAP EPS of $4.25 was impacted by a $0.17 non-cash goodwill impairment in the legacy Publishing & Advertising unit. Non-GAAP results clean with no material adjustments. Cash flow from operations of $2.17B was robust relative to GAAP net income of $1.71B. Share repurchases continued (8.5M shares).

**4. Management tone and key transcript highlights**  
Tone remained positive on AI demand and record results, with explicit raise to FY targets. However, the abrupt CFO departure (effective June 15) and ongoing CEO succession create noise. Management acknowledged freemium expansion and deferred price hikes will pressure near-term ARR growth in exchange for user scale. AI metrics (ARR tripling, Firefly consumption) cited as key positives.

**5. Thesis impact**  
Results reinforce the core subscription and AI growth drivers. Leadership instability and the decision to prioritize user growth over immediate monetization introduce short-term execution and visibility risks that were not previously factored into expectations.

**6. Recommendation: Accumulate**  
The beat-and-raise, AI ARR momentum, and clean cash generation outweigh the CFO exit and freemium commentary. Stock reaction creates an entry point to increase the 2.3% position at lower levels.

**Recommendation: Accumulate**
