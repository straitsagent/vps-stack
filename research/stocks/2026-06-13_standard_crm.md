**Business Overview**

Salesforce, Inc. is the global leader in customer relationship management (CRM) technology, operating the Agentforce 360 Platform that unifies sales, service, marketing, commerce, data, and AI agents on a single intelligent system. Founded in 1999, the company helps organizations become “agentic enterprises” by embedding autonomous AI agents (via Agentforce) that reason, learn, and act alongside humans. Core offerings include Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, Commerce Cloud, Slack, MuleSoft, Tableau, and Data 360. Revenue is generated primarily through subscription and support contracts sold directly and via partners, with flexibility for rapid configuration and integration. [1][2]

Key growth levers include multi-service adoption, deeper enterprise and international penetration, vertical-specific solutions, and generative/agentic AI investment. Recent acquisitions (Qualified in April 2026, Informatica in November 2025, Regrello in October 2025) accelerate the agentic roadmap by adding data management and workflow capabilities. Management is simultaneously executing restructuring to lower operating expenses while funding AI innovation. [2]

**Financial Position**

Salesforce delivered FY2026 revenue of $41.52 billion, up from $37.90 billion in FY2025 and $34.86 billion in FY2024, reflecting roughly 10% compound annual growth. Gross profit reached $32.26 billion (77.7% margin), operating income $8.92 billion, and net income $7.46 billion (18% net margin). EBITDA of $12.55 billion supported free cash flow of $14.40 billion, with operating cash flow at $15.00 billion. Trailing P/E stands at 19.2x and forward P/E at 10.7x; EV/EBITDA is 12.9x on a trailing basis. Market capitalization is $152.1 billion at recent prices near $166. [Financials table]

Balance sheet strength includes $112.31 billion in assets and $59.14 billion in equity, though total debt rose to $17.18 billion (Debt/Equity 0.3) and net debt/EBITDA reached 0.78. Current ratio of 0.76 reflects working-capital dynamics typical of subscription businesses. ROE (DuPont) improved to 0.13 from 0.07 in FY2024. The company maintains an aggressive capital return program, including a $25 billion accelerated share repurchase that reduced diluted shares from 970 million to 871 million. [Financials; 6]

**Competitive Position**

Salesforce holds approximately 20% of the global CRM market, the top position for the 13th consecutive year according to IDC data. Its moat derives from the breadth of the Customer 360 platform, deep customer data integration, extensive partner ecosystem, and switching costs embedded in enterprise deployments. Agentforce’s agentic layer and recent data/AI acquisitions further differentiate the offering versus point solutions. [63]

Primary competitors include ServiceNow (workflow automation), Adobe (marketing/creative), Oracle (enterprise applications), and emerging AI-native platforms such as Palantir. Peers trade at significantly higher multiples (PLTR 142x P/E, APP 43x). Salesforce’s scale, installed base, and ability to bundle AI agents across existing subscriptions provide a durable advantage, though execution risk on monetizing “headless” agent usage remains. [Peer table; 16]

**Catalysts**

Near-term upside drivers center on Agentforce adoption and AI monetization. Agentforce ARR reached $1.2 billion with 111% sequential growth in agentic work units; deals using three or more AI products grew nearly 70% year-over-year. The company guided Q2 FY2027 revenue of $11.21–11.40 billion and next earnings on September 2, 2026. Additional catalysts include continued share repurchases, potential organic growth re-acceleration in the second half of FY2027, and further vertical/healthcare wins (e.g., expanded CVS Health deployment of Agentforce Health). [53; 14; 60]

**Risks**

The stock has declined approximately 35% year-to-date and trades near the low end of its 52-week range ($161.40–$276.80), reflecting investor skepticism about AI monetization pace and software spending. Recent layoffs in Agentforce, MuleSoft, and Marketing Cloud units signal ongoing restructuring pressure. Competitive threats from AI-native tools and peers (Adobe, ServiceNow) could erode pricing power. Macro headwinds in enterprise IT budgets and elevated net debt after acquisitions add execution risk. Valuation remains sensitive to growth re-acceleration; activist exits (Starboard) and mixed analyst sentiment underscore downside volatility. [6; 11; 65; 66]
