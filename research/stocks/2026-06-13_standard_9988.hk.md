**1. Business Overview**

Alibaba Group Holding Limited operates China’s largest e-commerce ecosystem and a growing cloud infrastructure business. Its core segments include Alibaba China E-Commerce (Taobao, Tmall, 1688, Xianyu, and Taobao Instant Commerce), Alibaba International Digital Commerce (AliExpress, Lazada, Trendyol, Daraz, Alibaba.com), Cloud Intelligence Group, and All Others (Cainiao logistics, Freshippo, Amap, Youku). The company serves merchants and consumers through platform economics, logistics, and technology infrastructure, with 131,462 employees headquartered in Hangzhou.

Revenue is driven primarily by China commerce (customer management services and commissions), supplemented by cloud subscriptions and international retail/wholesale. In FY2026, group revenue reached RMB 1,023.67 billion, up from RMB 941.17 billion in FY2024. Quick commerce and cloud are the fastest-growing areas, with quick commerce revenue rising 47% YoY to RMB 78.52 billion and cloud external revenue accelerating to 40% YoY in the March 2026 quarter.

The business model combines marketplace commissions, advertising, and logistics fees in China with subscription-based cloud services and cross-border GMV. Recent emphasis has shifted toward high-frequency local commerce and AI-enhanced cloud offerings, including the Accio Work agentic platform.

**2. Financial Position**

Alibaba trades at a trailing P/E of 17.3x and forward P/E of 11.4x, with P/B at 1.7x and EV/EBITDA near 19.0x. ROE stands at just 0.1% for FY2026, reflecting heavy investment spending. Revenue grew modestly from RMB 941.17 billion (FY2024) to RMB 1,023.67 billion (FY2026), a 0% YoY print in the latest period, while gross profit rose to RMB 407.53 billion.

Operating income fell sharply to RMB 59.66 billion in FY2026 from RMB 147.08 billion the prior year, and net income declined to RMB 103.59 billion. Adjusted EBITA in the March quarter collapsed 84% YoY to RMB 5.1 billion as AI and quick-commerce capex surged. Operating cash flow dropped to RMB 76.21 billion from RMB 182.59 billion, with free cash flow turning negative (–RMB 50.72 billion) after capex reached RMB 126.94 billion.

Balance sheet metrics show rising leverage: total debt increased to RMB 281.72 billion (debt/equity 25.0), net debt reached RMB 150.19 billion, and net debt/EBITDA stood at 0.81x. Cash reserves declined to RMB 131.53 billion while the current ratio fell to 1.28. Margins compressed, with net margin at 10% versus 13% in FY2025.

**3. Competitive Position**

Alibaba retains a strong moat in China’s e-commerce through its integrated ecosystem of Taobao/Tmall, logistics (Cainiao), and payments, commanding the largest GMV share. However, this position is under pressure in high-frequency local commerce and instant delivery, where Meituan and JD.com are aggressive rivals. The reported $1.5 billion Pupu bid underscores the fight for grocery and 30-minute delivery share against Meituan’s Dingdong Fresh move and JD’s network.

In cloud, Alibaba holds a leading domestic position with 38% YoY revenue growth and AI products now 30% of external cloud revenue, yet it competes with Huawei Cloud, Tencent, and global hyperscalers. International operations (AliExpress, Lazada) remain subscale relative to domestic and face local competitors plus regulatory hurdles. Overall market share in core China retail is stable but eroding in newer verticals as rivals invest heavily in subsidies and infrastructure.

**4. Catalysts**

Near-term upside may come from continued cloud momentum, with external revenue growth at 40% and management targeting >RMB 30 billion in MaaS revenue by December 2026. AI-related product revenue has posted 11 consecutive quarters of triple-digit growth. The potential Pupu acquisition would expand Alibaba’s instant commerce footprint and challenge Meituan directly in groceries.

International commerce showed narrowing losses (adjusted EBITA loss reduced to RMB 138 million) and steady 5–9% revenue growth. A regular annual dividend of US$1.05 per ADS (ex-date June 2026) provides shareholder return support. Analyst consensus target of HK$183.48 implies material upside from recent levels near HK$110–126.

**5. Risks**

Execution risk is elevated as heavy AI and quick-commerce investments have driven free cash flow negative and collapsed quarterly adjusted EBITA. Competitive threats from Meituan, JD.com, and PDD Holdings in local services, plus cloud rivals, could sustain margin pressure. Macro headwinds include softening China consumer demand and retail sales slowdown.

Regulatory risks are acute: Beijing’s SAMR summoned platforms over “618” festival promotions, while the U.S. Pentagon added Alibaba to its Chinese military companies list, raising capital-market and compliance concerns. Geopolitical tensions and potential retaliation could further weigh on valuation, already reflected in the depressed P/B of 1.7x and low ROE.
