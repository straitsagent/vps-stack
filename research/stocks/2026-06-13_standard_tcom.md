**1. Business Overview**

Trip.com Group Limited operates as a leading online travel agency (OTA) providing accommodation reservations, transportation ticketing (air, train, bus, ferry), packaged tours, corporate travel management, and ancillary services such as insurance and in-destination activities. It functions primarily as an agent, earning commissions on bookings rather than owning inventory, across brands including Ctrip, Qunar, Trip.com, Skyscanner, and Travelfusion. The company serves customers in China and internationally, with a growing emphasis on outbound travel and corporate solutions via Trip.Biz. [42]

Key revenue drivers include hotel and accommodation bookings, air ticketing, and packaged tours, which together dominate the mix. The business model leverages a digital platform with high repeat usage, supported by user data for personalized recommendations. International expansion through acquisitions and partnerships has diversified revenue beyond domestic China travel, which remains the core contributor. [39]

Recent results show net revenue reaching RMB 62.4 billion in 2025, up 17% year-over-year, with Q4 2025 net revenue at RMB 15.4 billion (+21% YoY). The platform continues to invest in AI-driven tools for trip planning and supplier integration to enhance conversion and engagement. [39]

**2. Financial Position**

Trip.com reported strong top-line expansion in recent fiscal years, with revenue rising from $44.51 billion in 2023 to $53.29 billion in 2024 and $62.41 billion in 2025. Gross profit followed a similar trajectory, reaching $50.29 billion in 2025, while operating income climbed to $17.11 billion. Net income surged to $33.29 billion in 2025 from $9.92 billion in 2023, aided by investment gains and operating leverage, producing EPS of 50.62. [Income Statement]

Balance sheet metrics reflect a solid position: total assets grew to $267.39 billion by end-2025, with equity at $170.95 billion. Total debt declined to $31.61 billion (Debt/Equity 0.2), resulting in negative net debt of -$8.23 billion and Net Debt/EBITDA of -0.20. Cash stood at $39.85 billion. Valuation appears compressed relative to growth history: trailing P/E of 6.6x, P/B of 1.2x, and EV/EBITDA of -0.6x contrast with low reported ROE of 0.2% and ROIC of 0.2%. Net margins expanded to 0.53 in 2025 from 0.22 in 2023. Current ratio improved to 1.55, indicating adequate liquidity. [Valuation; Financial Health]

**3. Competitive Position**

Trip.com holds a dominant position in China’s OTA market through its scale, brand portfolio, and integrated ecosystem covering domestic and outbound travel. Its moat stems from network effects, extensive supplier relationships, and data advantages that support higher conversion rates and repeat bookings. International assets such as Skyscanner provide exposure outside China and differentiate it from purely domestic players. [42]

Key competitors include global OTAs such as Booking Holdings and Expedia, alongside local or regional players. In peer comparisons, Trip.com’s market capitalization of $30 billion far exceeds smaller travel names like HPL or Banyan Tree, reflecting its leadership in high-volume Asian travel segments. [Peer Comparison]

The company’s one-stop platform and corporate travel offerings create switching costs, while ongoing AI investments aim to widen the technology gap versus rivals. Market share gains in international OTA segments have been noted alongside domestic strength. [6]

**4. Catalysts**

Upcoming Q1 2026 earnings on June 24, 2026, with guided revenue of $15.76–15.97 billion, represent a near-term catalyst as investors assess continued momentum. Consensus estimates project full-year revenue growth around 19%, supported by normalized travel demand. [2; 1]

International expansion and AI initiatives, highlighted at the Envision.2026 conference, include new partnerships, MOUs with overseas entities, and tools such as Trip.Planner to drive cross-border bookings. China’s tourism recovery and outbound travel normalization provide macro tailwinds. [56; 39]

Analyst price targets average $77.05–77.10, implying substantial upside from recent prices near $46–48. Share repurchase programs and capital returns further support sentiment. [Valuation; 39]

**5. Risks**

Execution risk around international margin dilution persists, as higher investments in overseas markets have pressured profitability in recent quarters. Regulatory scrutiny, including ongoing China antitrust matters, could constrain growth or impose costs. [53; 22]

Macro headwinds such as China economic softness, currency fluctuations, or renewed travel restrictions may dampen booking volumes. Competitive intensity from global OTAs and potential shifts in consumer preferences toward direct bookings remain threats. [16]

Valuation risk is elevated if growth moderates; the stock has already declined notably from 52-week highs near $79 amid estimate revisions and broader market moves. Low short interest (2.4%) offers limited cushion against negative surprises. [Valuation; 1]
