**Business Overview**

CoreWeave operates a purpose-built cloud platform optimized for AI workloads, delivering GPU clusters, high-speed networking, AI-native storage (including LOTA), and proprietary orchestration software such as CoreWeave Mission Control and Slurm on Kubernetes. Revenue is generated primarily through committed, take-or-pay contracts for training and inference capacity rather than on-demand usage, with services spanning infrastructure, managed software, and developer tools including Weights & Biases. The company differentiates through first-to-market GPU deployments and vertically integrated automation that reduces time-to-steady-state performance for large distributed AI jobs. [1][2]

Key revenue drivers include hyperscale AI labs and enterprises seeking specialized high-density compute unavailable in general-purpose clouds. The platform supports the full AI lifecycle from model training to agentic workflows. As of early 2026, CoreWeave reported a contracted backlog of approximately $99.4 billion, including a $21 billion commitment from Meta extending through 2032. [29][51]

**Financial Position**

CoreWeave delivered explosive top-line growth, with revenue rising from $0.23 billion in 2023 to $1.92 billion in 2024 and $5.13 billion in 2025. Gross profit reached $3.68 billion in 2025 (71.7% margin), yet operating income turned negative at -$0.05 billion and net loss widened to -$1.17 billion (EPS -$2.75). EBITDA improved to $2.47 billion, but free cash flow remained deeply negative at -$7.25 billion due to $10.31 billion in capex. [Financial tables]

The balance sheet shows aggressive leverage: total debt of $29.82 billion, net debt of $26.70 billion, and debt/equity of 6.5x. Liquidity is tight with a current ratio of 0.46 and cash of $3.13 billion. Valuation metrics reflect growth expectations—P/B of 10.7–11.2x, EV/revenue of 14.1x, and EV/EBITDA of 25.9–29.0x—while trailing P/E is N/A due to losses. Recent financing includes a $3.1 billion DDTL facility and $3.5 billion senior notes (9.625% USD / 8.5% EUR due 2032) to fund GPU purchases. [3][5]

**Competitive Position**

CoreWeave’s moat stems from specialized AI infrastructure and software that general-purpose providers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) were not designed to match at equivalent density and cost. It consistently brings new GPU generations online faster than competitors and offers integrated orchestration that improves utilization for complex training jobs. Nvidia’s equity investment and partnership reinforce supply access and credibility. [1][45]

Direct peers in the provided table (Cloudflare, Snowflake, Twilio, MongoDB) operate in adjacent software or general cloud segments; CoreWeave competes more directly with other GPU cloud specialists and the GPU-as-a-service arms of hyperscalers. Market share data is limited, but its $99+ billion backlog and role as a top independent AI cloud provider indicate meaningful positioning among non-hyperscale capacity suppliers. High customer concentration risk exists, though credit quality has improved with investment-grade exposure rising. [15][57]

**Catalysts**

Inclusion in the Nasdaq-100 effective June 22, 2026, will drive passive inflows from index funds and ETFs. The $99.4 billion backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility, supported by large commitments from Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Jane Street. Recent product launches targeting agentic AI workflows and runtime acceleration could expand addressable spend per customer. [7][9][51][60]

Additional near-term drivers include continued hyperscaler AI capex tailwinds, potential further debt or equity raises at improving terms (evidenced by the oversubscribed DDTL facility), and operating leverage as new data centers reach steady-state utilization. Analyst consensus target of $140.18 implies ~33% upside from recent prices near $100. [Earnings calendar]

**Risks**

Execution risk is elevated given $10+ billion annual capex, negative free cash flow, and the need to bring leased and owned facilities online on schedule. High leverage (net debt/EBITDA 10.82x) and recent high-cost notes (up to 9.625%) create refinancing and interest expense pressure if AI demand moderates. Short interest of 18.2% and repeated insider sales (including CEO and CFO transactions in June 2026) signal skepticism. [3][15][66][68]

Valuation risk is material at 11x book and 14x revenue with still-negative net margins and ROE. Customer concentration, potential delays in GPU availability, and competition from better-capitalized hyperscalers represent additional threats. Macro headwinds from higher-for-longer rates could further stress the capital-intensive model.
