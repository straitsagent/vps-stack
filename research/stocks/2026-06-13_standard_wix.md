**Business Overview**

Wix.com Ltd. operates a cloud-based web development platform enabling users to create, customize, and manage websites through drag-and-drop tools. Core offerings include the Wix Editor for individual creators, Wix Studio for agencies and advanced sites, Velo for developers, and integrated business solutions such as Payments by Wix, commerce tools, bookings, and memberships. Revenue is generated primarily through premium subscriptions (creative subscriptions segment) and business solutions including payments and marketing add-ons. The company serves millions of users globally, with recent emphasis on AI-powered features like Base44 (acquired AI app builder) and the proprietary Harmony LLM for automated design and content generation. [53][67]

The SaaS model follows a freemium approach, converting free users to paid tiers while monetizing via higher-value services such as e-commerce and enterprise tools. In 2025, total revenue reached $1.99B, up from $1.76B in 2024 and $1.56B in 2023, driven by subscription growth and payments adoption. Recent initiatives include Wix Headless integration with Stripe Projects for backend provisioning and expansion of AI agents for site creation. [1][39]

**Financial Position**

Wix reported revenue of $541.2M in Q1 2026 (+14% YoY), with creative subscriptions at $382.4M (+13%) and business solutions at $158.8M (+17%). However, non-GAAP EPS fell to $0.68 from $1.55 YoY, missing estimates amid elevated operating expenses (+49% YoY). Full-year 2025 revenue grew to $1.99B with gross profit of $1.36B (68% margin), but operating income was near breakeven and net income $0.05B. Trailing EPS turned negative at -$0.72. [15][43]

Balance sheet metrics show strain: total debt rose to $1.59B (Debt/Equity 6.3x), equity turned negative at -$0.37B, and net debt/EBITDA reached 35x. Cash stood at $0.31B. Offsetting this, operating cash flow improved to $0.58B and free cash flow to $0.57B in 2025, supported by subscription collections. Valuation multiples remain elevated (forward P/E 6.0x, P/S 0.9x) despite N/A trailing P/E and negative ROE (-0.6%), with market cap at $2.2B after a sharp price decline. [DB Context]

**Competitive Position**

Wix competes in the website builder and e-commerce platform market against Shopify (higher forward P/S of 8.9x and stronger execution), Squarespace, and WordPress/WooCommerce. Its moat rests on ease-of-use for non-technical users and an integrated ecosystem of apps, payments, and now AI tools. However, market share pressure is evident as developers and agencies shift toward specialized AI solutions, contributing to a slowdown in the partners segment. [5][11][69]

Wix holds an estimated position powering millions of sites with $12B+ in annual e-commerce volume, but trails Shopify in enterprise and B2B growth. Differentiation via Wix Studio and Velo targets agencies, yet AI commoditization risks eroding pricing power. Short interest at 25.8% reflects skepticism on sustained differentiation. [49][67]

**Catalysts**

Near-term upside may stem from the 20% workforce reduction expected to deliver $70M in annual savings and the $250M capital raise to fund AI initiatives. Integration of Wix Headless with Stripe Projects and rollout of Harmony AI features could accelerate developer adoption and ARPU. Management highlighted partner network quality improvements and potential buybacks as supports. [1][15][18]

Q2 2026 revenue guidance of $0.55–0.56B and analyst targets averaging $81–86 (versus current ~$46) imply recovery potential if AI monetization materializes and cost discipline holds. FCF margins above 30% provide flexibility. [DB Context][52]

**Risks**

Execution risk is elevated following the Q1 2026 earnings miss and subsequent cut to 2026 revenue guidance (now low- to mid-teens growth, down $25M) plus $50M bookings reduction, attributed to restructuring and partner softness. High debt load and negative equity raise refinancing concerns amid macro uncertainty. [10][60][64]

Competitive threats from AI-native tools and Shopify’s scale could further pressure growth, while 25.8% short interest and repeated analyst target cuts (e.g., RBC to $45, Wells Fargo to $47) signal valuation risk despite the recent drop. Geopolitical exposure as an Israel-based firm adds volatility. [6][9][11]
