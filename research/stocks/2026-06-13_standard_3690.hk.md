**1. Business Overview**

Meituan operates as a technology-driven local commerce platform in China and select international markets, connecting consumers with merchants primarily through its app ecosystem. The company runs two main segments: Core Local Commerce, which includes food delivery, in-store dining, hotel and travel bookings, and related marketing services; and New Initiatives, encompassing bike/e-moped sharing, grocery delivery, power banks, micro-credit, and overseas expansion such as the Keeta platform in Saudi Arabia. With 111,298 employees and headquarters in Beijing, Meituan monetizes traffic via commissions on transactions, advertising, and value-added merchant services while managing logistics through an algorithm-optimized rider network.

Key revenue drivers center on high-frequency food delivery and in-store services, which generate the bulk of Core Local Commerce revenue through order commissions and promotions. New Initiatives contribute growing but lower-margin revenue from grocery retail and international operations. The business model relies on network effects: dense urban user bases and rider fleets enable efficient matching, with data and AI enhancing dispatch and personalization. Recent quarterly revenue reached CNY 91 billion in Q1 2026, reflecting 5.6% YoY growth despite competitive pressures.

**2. Financial Position**

Meituan’s financials show consistent top-line expansion alongside recent profitability deterioration. Revenue rose from USD 276.74 billion in 2023 to 337.59 billion in 2024 and 364.85 billion in 2025, a compound annual growth rate exceeding 15%. However, gross profit declined to 111.01 billion in 2025 from 129.78 billion the prior year, with operating income swinging to a loss of 29.84 billion and net income to a 23.36 billion loss (EPS -3.84). Trailing metrics reflect this: P/E is N/A, P/B stands at 2.8x, EV/EBITDA at -10.5x, and ROE at -0.2% (or -24.09% on a TTM basis per some reports). Net margin turned negative at -6% in 2025 versus +11% in 2024.

Balance sheet strength remains evident with total assets of 346.91 billion, cash of 106.77 billion, and net debt of -20.15 billion (net cash position) despite total debt rising to 86.62 billion and debt/equity at 75.3. Operating cash flow turned negative at -13.82 billion in 2025, with free cash flow at -27.09 billion amid elevated capex of 13.27 billion. Current ratio holds at 1.82. Q1 2026 results showed a narrowed net loss of CNY 6.83 billion on revenue of CNY 91 billion, with adjusted losses also improving sequentially as subsidy intensity moderated.

**3. Competitive Position**

Meituan holds a leading position in China’s on-demand food delivery market but faces eroding advantages from aggressive rivals. Its moat stems from scale in users, merchants, and riders, plus proprietary logistics algorithms and data advantages that support faster delivery and higher order frequency. However, this has been challenged by subsidy-driven competition, reducing unit economics and market share stability in grocery and instant retail.

Key competitors include JD.com, which entered food delivery and triggered broader price wars, and Alibaba, which is bidding USD 1.5 billion for grocery platform Pupu to expand local commerce reach. Meituan has countered with its own USD 717 million acquisition of Dingdong Fresh. Market dynamics show three dominant players consolidating scarce assets amid regulatory scrutiny of “involution” practices. While Meituan retains leadership in mid-to-high average order value segments, its position in pure grocery remains contested, and overseas initiatives like Keeta add new competitive fronts.

**4. Catalysts**

Near-term upside may arise from regulatory efforts to curb destructive price competition. China’s SAMR has held meetings on fair competition, and state media commentary has signaled reduced tolerance for irrational subsidies, potentially allowing unit economics to recover. Goldman Sachs noted visible profit recovery in food delivery, forecasting possible break-even as early as Q2 2026 with per-order profit potentially reaching RMB 1.

Additional drivers include continued narrowing of Core Local Commerce losses, resilient user growth post-subsidy rationalization, and expansion in grocery and overseas markets. AI integration across operations and supply chain investments could further improve efficiency. Analyst consensus targets of approximately HKD 108.89 (from 37 analysts) imply material upside from recent prices near HKD 78, supported by revenue guidance of CNY 91–103 billion for upcoming periods.

**5. Risks**

Execution risks are elevated as Meituan invests heavily in New Initiatives and overseas expansion while absorbing losses from price wars. Continued or renewed subsidy battles with JD.com and Alibaba could further compress margins, as seen in the shift to three consecutive quarterly losses through Q1 2026. Macro headwinds in China, including weaker consumer spending and regulatory tightening on tech platforms, add pressure.

Valuation risks stem from N/A trailing P/E, forward P/E of 16.4x amid ongoing losses, and a stock that has declined significantly year-to-date. High capex and negative free cash flow may strain resources if growth slows. Insider transactions, primarily routine director sales, and low institutional ownership visibility also warrant monitoring. Overall, the combination of competitive intensity and China-specific policy uncertainty could limit near-term re-rating despite improving sequential trends.
