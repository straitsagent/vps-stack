**1. Business Overview**

Baidu operates two segments: Baidu General Business (mobile ecosystem, AI cloud, and intelligent driving) and iQIYI (online video). The core Baidu App delivers search, feed, and knowledge products to ~655 million MAUs, while ERNIE Bot serves as its conversational AI assistant. Apollo Go provides autonomous ride-hailing, and DuerOS powers smart assistants. iQIYI contributes professionally produced video content. In FY25, Baidu General Business accounted for 79% of revenue and iQIYI 21%.

Revenue remains anchored in online marketing, but the mix is shifting rapidly. AI-powered businesses reached 52% of Baidu General revenue in 1Q26 (+49% YoY), crossing the 50% threshold for the first time. Cloud infrastructure revenue jumped 79% YoY to RMB 8.8 billion, with GPU cloud revenue surging 184%. Management highlights full-stack AI capabilities, including proprietary Kunlunxin chips, as the new growth engine.

**2. Financial Position**

Revenue declined from RMB 134.60 billion in 2023 to 133.12 billion in 2024 and 129.08 billion in 2025. Gross profit fell to 56.64 billion and operating income halved to 10.37 billion. Net income dropped to 5.59 billion (net margin 4%), with ROE at 0.0% in the latest period versus 8-9% previously. Operating cash flow turned negative at -3.01 billion and free cash flow at -16.43 billion after elevated capex of 13.42 billion.

Balance sheet metrics show rising leverage: total debt increased to 97.08 billion, net debt/EBITDA reached 2.35x, and debt/equity stood at 32.2. The current ratio weakened to 1.76 from 3.01. Valuation appears compressed with trailing P/E unavailable, forward P/E at 13.0x, P/B at 1.0x, and EV/EBITDA at 13.4x. Analyst targets average around HKD 180 versus recent prices near 115-130.

**3. Competitive Position**

Baidu retains leadership in Chinese mobile search and holds a strong position in AI development relative to domestic peers. Its full-stack approach—spanning ERNIE models, Kunlunxin chips, and Apollo autonomy—differentiates it from Tencent and Alibaba in the AI cloud and intelligent driving segments. Apollo Go operates in 27 cities with international expansion underway, while iQIYI maintains scale in video but faces broader streaming competition.

Market share advantages are clearest in AI inference demand and proprietary hardware. Cloud infrastructure growth outpaces legacy ad businesses, and management targets 25-30% gross margins on cloud infra through chip optimization and pricing power. However, Baidu trails global hyperscalers and faces intensifying domestic competition in generative AI applications.

**4. Catalysts**

AI cloud infrastructure is the primary near-term driver, with 50%/40%/38% growth forecasts for FY26-28 and structural margin expansion. The 1Q26 earnings beat (revenue RMB 32.08 billion vs. 31.49 billion expected) confirmed the revenue mix inflection. Apollo Go’s European regulatory approval and continued China rollout add optionality, while the planned Kunlunxin IPO could unlock value.

Analyst revisions reflect optimism: Susquehanna raised its target to USD 110 on AI strength, and DBS maintains a BUY with HKD 187 target. Next earnings on 19 August 2026, with revenue guidance of RMB 32.11-34.68 billion, offer a near-term catalyst if AI momentum continues.

**5. Risks**

Geopolitical exposure intensified after Baidu’s addition to the U.S. Department of Defense Chinese Military Companies list, raising reputational and potential capital-market risks despite no immediate sanctions. Legacy online marketing continues to face structural pressure, contributing to the revenue decline and margin compression observed in FY25.

High capex and negative free cash flow in 2025 signal execution risk around AI investments. iQIYI revenue missed expectations in 1Q26, and broader China macro or regulatory tightening on tech platforms could weigh on ad spending. Valuation risk exists if AI monetization disappoints relative to the 13x forward P/E and 1.0x P/B multiples.
