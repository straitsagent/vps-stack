**1. Business Overview**

Baidu operates primarily through two segments: Baidu General Business (core operations) and iQIYI. The core segment delivers China’s leading search engine via the Baidu App, alongside Haokan short video, knowledge products (Wiki, Knows), ERNIE Bot conversational AI, DuerOS voice assistant, AI Cloud infrastructure, and Apollo Go autonomous driving/robotaxi services. iQIYI provides online video streaming. Revenue drivers include online marketing and advertising (still the majority but declining), AI Cloud services, intelligent driving, and content. The business model centers on a mobile ecosystem monetized through ads and value-added services, with accelerating investment in AI infrastructure and autonomous mobility to offset legacy search weakness.

In Q1 2026, Baidu General Business revenue reached RMB 26.0 billion, flat quarter-over-quarter, while AI Cloud Infra grew 79% YoY and GPU Cloud surged 184% YoY. Apollo Go delivered 3.2 million fully driverless rides (+120% YoY) and over 22 million cumulative rides across 27 cities. The company is shifting from a search/advertising platform toward an AI-first model, with AI-powered business revenue reaching RMB 13.6 billion (over 50% of General Business for the first time).

**3. Competitive Position**

Baidu holds a strong moat in Chinese-language search and AI data advantages, reinforced by ERNIE Bot and full-stack AI capabilities. Apollo Go leads in cumulative autonomous miles (330+ million, 220 million fully driverless) and operates in 27 cities. Market share remains dominant in domestic search and online marketing, though advertising faces pressure from peers. Key competitors include Alibaba and Tencent in cloud and ads, Kuaishou (1024.HK) in short video and social commerce, and global AV players such as Waymo. In AI Cloud, Baidu competes with domestic infrastructure providers while benefiting from enterprise demand for its differentiated stack.

The company’s position is supported by regulatory approvals for Level 4 operations abroad and partnerships (PostBus in Switzerland, Lyft in London), yet it faces intensifying competition in both legacy ads and the global robotaxi race.

**4. Catalysts**

Near-term upside includes continued AI Cloud momentum (79% YoY growth in Q1) and Apollo Go international expansion, highlighted by the June 2026 Level 4 permit from Switzerland’s FEDRO for AmiGo operations in eastern cantons and planned fully driverless public transport in early 2027. Additional European and UK trials (London with Lyft) broaden the addressable market. Analyst price targets average $180.51 (33 analysts), implying substantial upside from current levels. Potential first-time dividend policy and Kunlunxin-related valuation considerations have also drawn attention. Susquehanna raised its target to $140 on AI Cloud strength.

**5. Risks**

Execution risk stems from heavy AI CapEx driving negative free cash flow ($–16.43B in 2025) and margin pressure. The U.S. Department of Defense added Baidu to its Chinese Military Companies list in June 2026, triggering procurement restrictions from June 30, 2026, and potential wider limits by 2027, despite company statements that the designation imposes no immediate sanctions on commercial operations. Legacy online marketing declined 22% YoY, offsetting AI gains. Macro headwinds include U.S.–China policy uncertainty, Chinese regulatory scrutiny, and ad-market weakness. Valuation risk exists around the elevated trailing P/E and sensitivity to AI monetization timelines amid 4.8% short interest.
