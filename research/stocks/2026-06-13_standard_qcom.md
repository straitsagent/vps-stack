**1. Business Overview**  
QUALCOMM develops and commercializes foundational wireless and computing technologies through three segments: Qualcomm CDMA Technologies (QCT) for integrated circuits and system software, Qualcomm Technology Licensing (QTL) for intellectual property licensing, and Qualcomm Strategic Initiatives (QSI) for strategic investments. QCT supplies Snapdragon platforms for handsets, automotive (Snapdragon Digital Chassis for connectivity, digital cockpit, and ADAS/AD), and IoT applications including PCs, XR, edge networking, and industrial uses. QTL licenses cellular standard-essential patents (3G/4G/5G) and other IP. The company reported 52,000 employees and operates on a 52/53-week fiscal year ending in September. [1][2]

Revenue reached $44.28 billion in FY2025 (ended Sep 30, 2025), up from $38.96 billion in FY2024 and $35.82 billion in FY2023, driven primarily by QCT handset, automotive, and IoT sales plus QTL royalties. In Q2 FY2026 (ended ~Mar 2026), total revenue was $10.6 billion (–3% YoY), with QCT down 4% due to lower handset volumes partially offset by automotive and IoT growth, while QTL rose 5% on favorable mix and higher estimated revenue per unit. [2]

**2. Financial Position**  
FY2025 gross profit was $24.55 billion (55.4% margin) and operating income $12.39 billion, with EBITDA at $14.93 billion. Net income fell to $5.54 billion (EPS $5.05) from $10.14 billion in FY2024 due to the absence of prior-year tax benefits, though Q2 FY2026 net income surged to $7.4 billion after a $5.7 billion valuation allowance release tied to CAMT guidance. Operating cash flow reached $14.01 billion and free cash flow $12.82 billion in FY2025, supporting dividends and buybacks. [1][2]

Balance sheet metrics show total assets of $50.14 billion, equity $21.21 billion, and total debt $14.81 billion (Debt/Equity 0.7) at Sep 30, 2025. Net debt stood at $9.29 billion with Net Debt/EBITDA of 0.62x and current ratio 2.82x. Trailing P/E is 22.8x, forward P/E 19.8x, P/B 8.2x, and EV/EBITDA 17.6x. Market cap is $227.6 billion; analyst consensus target is $180.48 (below recent prices near $212). ROE (DuPont) was 0.26 in FY2025. [Financials table; Valuation table]

**3. Competitive Position**  
Qualcomm holds a strong moat via its QTL patent portfolio covering essential wireless standards and its QCT leadership in mobile application processors. It maintains significant share in smartphone AP-SoCs and is expanding in automotive and edge AI. Key competitors include Broadcom and Marvell in connectivity, AMD and Nvidia in AI/compute, and Intel/Texas Instruments across semis. Peers trade at higher multiples (AMD 169.4x P/E, MU 46.3x) despite QCOM’s diversified end markets. [Peer table]

Customer vertical integration (e.g., Apple modems) and handset OEM concentration pose risks, but diversification into automotive (now >$6B annualized run rate) and data center custom silicon reduces reliance on smartphones. Recent Nvidia RTX Spark competition in Windows on Arm PCs highlights edge-AI rivalry, yet Qualcomm’s on-device AI focus (low-power inference) differentiates it from data-center-centric peers. [7][10]

**4. Catalysts**  
Near-term drivers include the June 24 Investor Day, where management is expected to detail data-center targets exceeding $3 billion in FY2027 and potentially $35 billion by FY2031 via AI100 Ultra (now on AWS) and custom ASICs with hyperscalers, with initial shipments pulled into calendar 2026. Automotive revenue hit a record $1.33 billion in Q2 (+38% YoY) and is projected to exit FY2026 above a $6 billion run rate. IoT and AI PC (Snapdragon X/C series) momentum, plus macro rate relief from lower oil/yields, have supported recent share gains. Wells Fargo raised its target to $230. [7][18][56]

Q3 FY2026 guidance implies sequential stability, with next earnings on July 29. On-device AI demand across handsets, PCs, and robotics provides additional tailwinds beyond traditional handset cycles. [2][72]

**5. Risks**  
Execution risk on data-center ramp is material given ambitious long-term targets and competition from Nvidia/AMD. Handset revenue remains pressured by memory supply constraints and Chinese OEM inventory drawdowns, with Q2 showing 4% QCT decline. China exposure, U.S.–China trade tensions, and customer vertical integration (Apple) are highlighted in forward-looking statements. Valuation risk exists with shares trading above the $180.48 analyst target and 22.8x trailing P/E amid volatile semis multiples. Short interest is modest at 4.8% but insider sales have occurred. [1][2][7]

Macro headwinds or delayed hyperscaler adoption could pressure growth forecasts, while high beta (1.60x) amplifies market swings. Net margin compression outside one-time tax items warrants monitoring. [Financials; Valuation]
