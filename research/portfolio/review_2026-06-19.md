**Portfolio Weekly Commentary – Week Ending 19 June 2026**

The portfolio declined by 0.52% for the week, closing at $1,038,998, driven primarily by sharp losses in Chinese internet names and a significant de-rating in Salesforce. While semiconductor and AI infrastructure holdings provided a powerful counterbalance, the week’s performance underscores the portfolio’s acute sensitivity to idiosyncratic regulatory and competitive shocks, particularly within its concentrated Communication Services and Consumer Cyclical exposures.

**1. Notable Movers and News Catalysts**

The most significant drag was **Alibaba Group (9988.HK and BABA)**, which collectively contributed roughly -$10,300 in losses. The Hong Kong-listed shares fell 10.4%, while the ADR dropped 7.2%. The proximate cause was a confluence of negative headlines: a report that Alibaba had bid $1.5 billion for a Chinese grocer to compete with Meituan, signaling a renewed price war in food delivery; fresh Chinese regulations governing subsidies in the sector; and broader concerns about China’s $300 billion pile of bad consumer debt. The divergence between the HK-listed stock and the ADR suggests additional selling pressure from local institutional investors or index rebalancing. **Meituan (3690.HK)** fell 10.2% on the same regulatory and competitive dynamics, reinforcing that the portfolio’s China exposure is concentrated in a fragile regulatory environment.

**Salesforce (CRM)** was the worst-performing US holding, collapsing 18.2% and costing the portfolio $3,388. The trigger was a fundamental shift in its pricing model: Salesforce is moving from per-seat licensing to usage-based “Agentforce” pricing. While management framed this as a growth catalyst, the market interpreted it as a sign that enterprise AI agent adoption is commoditizing faster than expected, threatening Salesforce’s historical pricing power. The news that Oracle outperformed Salesforce in the same earnings window amplified the rotation out of CRM.

On the positive side, **AMD** surged 15.2%, adding $3,550, driven by a rotation from cloud platforms to semiconductor stocks. News that cloud infrastructure spending is intensifying, with AMD specifically benefiting from AI compute demand, was a clear catalyst. **Applied Materials (AMAT)** rose 11.7% on continued AI chip manufacturing demand, though a headline noting its valuation now exceeds dot-com era peaks warrants caution. **Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM)** gained 8.3%, supported by its passing of the Minervini Trend Template screen and Apple’s resilience despite memory cost pressures. **Qualcomm (QCOM)** added 11.4% on strong momentum, though a separate analysis suggested the stock may be 34% overvalued. **CoreWeave (CRWV)** rose 9.2% on its Nasdaq-100 inclusion and record AI training results, validating the neocloud thesis.
