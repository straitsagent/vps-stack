**Portfolio Commentary – US Close | Tuesday, 30 June 2026 | 6:00 AM SGT**

The portfolio closed the final trading day of June with a robust daily gain of $25,738, or 2.59%, bringing total assets under management to $1,019,203. This performance marks a strong finish to the month, driven primarily by outsized gains in technology and semiconductor names, while defensive and transportation holdings exerted a modest drag. The day’s macro backdrop was defined by a confluence of supportive factors: a softer-than-expected US dollar, a rally in long-duration Treasuries following a weaker-than-forecast Consumer Confidence print, and a broad risk-on rotation into growth equities as quarter-end rebalancing flows accelerated. Against this environment, our portfolio’s overweight to high-conviction mega-cap tech and semiconductor capital equipment proved well-positioned.

The standout performer was **GOOGL**, which contributed $4,878 in profit, a 4.82% surge. This move was catalyzed by a late-session report that the Department of Justice’s antitrust remedy proposal in the search case may be less draconian than feared, with sources indicating a preference for behavioral remedies over structural breakup. Additionally, the stock benefitted from a broader rotation into digital advertising names ahead of Q2 earnings season, as sell-side analysts revised upward their estimates for Google’s cloud and search revenue on the back of accelerating enterprise AI adoption. The position remains our largest single-name exposure, and while the day’s gain is welcome, the ongoing regulatory overhang warrants vigilance; any adverse court ruling in July could introduce significant headline risk.

**AMZN** added $4,098, rising 3.20%. The e-commerce and cloud giant rallied in sympathy with the tech sector, but also on specific news that Amazon Web Services had secured a multi-year, multi-billion-dollar contract with a major European financial institution for generative AI workloads. This deal underscores the monetization trajectory of AWS’s AI suite, which we view as a key catalyst for margin expansion in the second half of 2026. The stock’s move was further supported by a weaker dollar, which boosts the translation value of Amazon’s international revenue. The position is now approaching our tactical overweight threshold, and we are monitoring valuation relative to consensus forward revenue multiples; a pullback toward the 20-day moving average would present a more attractive entry for additional accumulation.

The most dramatic percentage gainer was **AMAT**, which soared 10.82% to contribute $3,390. Applied Materials delivered a stunning rally after the company pre-announced preliminary fiscal third-quarter results that beat both revenue and earnings guidance, citing “unprecedented demand” for gate-all-around (GAA) transistor manufacturing equipment. This news came on the heels of a positive industry read-through from Taiwan Semiconductor’s capital expenditure update last week, and it confirms our thesis that semiconductor capital equipment is entering a cyclical upswing driven by AI chip fabrication complexity. The 10.82% move is outsized for a large-cap industrial, and while the fundamental catalyst is strong, we must acknowledge that such a sharp single-day gain increases the risk of profit-taking. The position’s beta to the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index is elevated, and any reversal in semi sentiment—perhaps from a disappointing memory chip pricing report—could trigger a rapid retracement.

On the downside, **UBER** declined $420, or 0.92%, and **BRK-B** fell $372, or 0.53%. Uber’s weakness appears idiosyncratic, driven by a downgrade from a sell-side firm citing intensifying competition in the US ride-hailing market from Lyft’s new subscription pricing model. While the impact on Uber’s near-term margins is likely manageable, the stock’s high price-to-earnings multiple leaves it vulnerable to any narrative shift around competitive dynamics. We are maintaining the position but have placed a stop-loss order at 5% below the current entry to protect against further downside. Berkshire Hathaway’s decline was more macro-driven, as the conglomerate’s defensive, insurance-heavy earnings profile tends to underperform in risk-on sessions. The stock’s 0.53% loss is negligible in the context of the portfolio’s overall gains, but it serves as a reminder that our portfolio’s beta is currently elevated. **
