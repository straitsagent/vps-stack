Applied Materials suffered even more acutely, dropping 8.48% and costing the portfolio $2,715. The 8.48% decline is the largest single-day drop for the name in over three months and broke through its 50-day moving average, a technical level that had held since April. NVIDIA, down 4.13% for a $2,583 loss, rounds out the semiconductor trifecta. While NVIDIA’s data-center GPU demand remains robust, the stock is increasingly correlated with the broader semi cycle, and today’s move reflects a de-rating of its forward P/E as the market reassesses the sustainability of its growth trajectory in a potentially softening enterprise spending environment.

In stark contrast, the portfolio’s defensive and non-cyclical positions provided a meaningful offset. BRK-B’s move is consistent with a risk-off rotation where capital seeks out balance-sheet strength and dividend stability over growth narratives.
