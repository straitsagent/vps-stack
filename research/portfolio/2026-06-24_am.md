The catalyst appeared to be a stronger-than-expected flash services PMI reading for June, which came in at 54.8 against a consensus of 53.2, suggesting that the service sector remains stubbornly resilient. The market repriced the probability of a September rate cut down to roughly 45%, from 58% just a week ago, sending the 2-year Treasury yield up 8 basis points to 4.72% and the 10-year yield to 4.38%. This rate shock was particularly damaging for long-duration growth equities, which explains the disproportionate pain in our semiconductor and Chinese ADR positions.

The most significant drag on the day came from our technology and semiconductor holdings, with TSM and AMAT leading the downside. The move was exacerbated by a combination of the macro rate repricing and a specific sector rotation out of AI-related plays. Applied Materials, which dropped 8.48% and cost the portfolio $2,715, was directly in the crosshairs of this narrative. The stock’s beta to the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index, which fell over 4%, amplified the damage. This is a clear risk factor to monitor: the semiconductor cycle is increasingly bifurcated between AI demand and traditional end-markets, and any policy noise from Washington regarding chip exports to China could trigger further volatility. Our position sizing in AMAT, while not outsized relative to the portfolio, is now under review given the elevated regulatory tail risk.

While the report remains unconfirmed, the market’s reaction underscores the persistent regulatory overhang on Chinese ADRs. The broader Hang Seng Index also fell 1.5% overnight, adding to the negative sentiment. Alibaba remains a high-conviction holding from a valuation perspective, trading at roughly 9x forward earnings, but the geopolitical and regulatory risk premium is clearly widening. We will need to assess whether the potential reward justifies the continued volatility, particularly as the US-China trade rhetoric intensifies ahead of the November elections.
