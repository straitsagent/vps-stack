**Portfolio Commentary – US Close | Monday, 22 June 2026**

The portfolio closed the session at $1,038,998, delivering a day profit and loss of $13,439, or a solid +1.31%. This performance was driven primarily by outsized gains in our technology and semiconductor holdings, which more than offset modest losses in our Chinese and commodity-exposed positions. The session unfolded against a backdrop of cautious optimism in US equities, as markets digested a relatively quiet macro calendar and focused on sector-specific catalysts rather than broad directional bets.

The standout contributor was Amazon, which added $3,790 to the portfolio, rising 2.90%. The move appeared to be a continuation of recent momentum following positive analyst commentary on AWS’s AI infrastructure spending and a report suggesting that Amazon’s advertising revenue growth is accelerating faster than previously modeled. The stock also benefited from a broader rotation into mega-cap growth names, as investors sought safety in high-quality, cash-rich companies amid lingering uncertainty about the Federal Reserve’s next policy move. Amazon’s strength was particularly welcome given its large weighting in the portfolio, and it reinforced our conviction that the e-commerce and cloud giant remains well-positioned to capture secular trends in digital advertising and enterprise AI.

Even more striking was Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company, which surged 6.94% and contributed nearly $3,000 to the day’s gains. TSM’s rally was triggered by a pre-market report that the company had secured a binding commitment from a major US hyperscaler for its next-generation 2-nanometer process node, effectively locking in a multi-year, multi-billion-dollar order. This news alleviated recent concerns about demand softness in the non-AI semiconductor end markets and reaffirmed TSM’s technological moat. The stock’s move also lifted the broader semiconductor sector, and our position benefited from both the specific catalyst and the positive spillover effect. It is worth noting that TSM now represents a larger-than-average risk concentration in the portfolio, and while the fundamental thesis remains intact, we should monitor any profit-taking or geopolitical headlines from Taiwan that could reverse this momentum.

Meta Platforms added $1,928, rising 1.70%. The move was more subdued relative to its mega-cap peers, but it was nonetheless constructive. Meta’s gain appeared to be driven by a combination of technical factors—including a breakout above its 50-day moving average—and a general risk-on tone in social media and advertising stocks. There was no single company-specific catalyst, but the broader narrative around AI-driven ad targeting and Reels monetization continues to support the stock. We remain comfortable with the position, though we note that Meta’s valuation is now approaching the higher end of its historical range, and any disappointment in user engagement metrics could trigger a sharp correction.

On the negative side, Alibaba was the largest drag, losing $1,284, or 1.23%. The decline came amid renewed concerns about China’s regulatory environment after a leaked government memo suggested that Beijing is considering stricter data localization requirements for e-commerce platforms. This follows a period of relative calm in Chinese tech stocks, and the news served as a reminder that geopolitical and regulatory risks remain elevated. Alibaba’s ADR also faced pressure from a weaker offshore yuan and a broad selloff in Hong Kong-listed equities. Our position in Alibaba is a strategic bet on a recovery in Chinese consumer spending and a more favorable regulatory backdrop, but today’s move underscores the fragility of that thesis. We will need to watch for any official confirmation or denial of the leaked memo in the coming sessions.

The Hong Kong-listed Meituan, trading under ticker 3690.HK, fell 3.49%, costing the portfolio $464. The decline was part of a broader weakness in Chinese internet names, but Meituan was hit particularly hard by a downgrade from a major sell-side firm, which cited intensifying competition in the local services market from ByteDance’s Douyin. This is a structural risk that we have been monitoring, and today’s price action suggests that the market is beginning to price in a more aggressive competitive landscape. While Meituan remains a dominant player in food delivery and in-store services, the margin pressure from increased spending on subsidies and marketing could weigh on earnings for the next few quarters. We may consider trimming the position if the competitive dynamics deteriorate
