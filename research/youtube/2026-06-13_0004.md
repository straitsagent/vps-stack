It explains this discrepancy through three key reasons: markets move on emotional expectations rather than fundamentals, recoveries from crashes happen in violent spikes rather than gradual gains, and the inherent volatility is the reason investors earn a risk premium in the first place. The analysis also highlights that entire decades can produce wildly different returns—from losing money in the 1930s and 2000s to doubling the average in the 1950s—which can define an investor’s entire career based on when they were born. The video concludes that while trying to time these unpredictable regimes is a costly mistake, the cyclical nature of euphoria and digestion decades is a historical pattern worth noting.
