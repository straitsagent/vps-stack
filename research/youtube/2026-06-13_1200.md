## Economist

The video explains the emergence of the "Cockroach Janta Party" in India, a youth-led movement sparked by a Supreme Court justice’s remark comparing unemployed young people to cockroaches. It began as an online joke but escalated into street protests, notably in Delhi, where demonstrators demanded the education minister’s resignation over two major exam scandals. The movement reflects broader frustration among young Indians about a lack of jobs and a broken system, with participants expressing a new willingness to criticize the government. While India has a history of street protests driving political change, the reporter notes this movement is still small (1,000–2,000 attendees) and lacks a strong leader, clear message, or sustained organizational capacity. The government appears confident it can weather the discontent, despite rising inflation and energy costs fueling broader public unease. The conclusion is cautious: the movement has generated significant online attention but faces major hurdles in becoming a lasting force for reform.
