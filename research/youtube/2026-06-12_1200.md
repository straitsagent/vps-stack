## Nanalyze
**[Understanding Tracking Error Simply](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YVbIfbKb8Og)**

The video explains that a passive fund or ETF aims to replicate the performance of its underlying benchmark. The "tracking error" is the small difference between the fund's actual returns and the benchmark's returns. This discrepancy arises due to factors like fund fees, cash holdings, and trading costs. The video emphasizes that while tracking error is inevitable, it is typically very small for well-managed funds. It concludes that investors should be aware of tracking error as a measure of fund efficiency but not overly concerned if it remains minimal. The recommendation is to choose low-cost funds with a history of closely tracking their benchmark.
