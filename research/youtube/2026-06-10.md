## Professor G
**[5 Laws Of Dividend ETF Placement (SCHD, JEPI, JEPQ, SPYI)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sk8HRftKCXQ)**

Based on the transcript, the video explains how the tax treatment of dividend ETFs varies by account type, and that holding the wrong fund in the wrong account can cost investors significant money over time. The speaker outlines five laws of placement, noting that SCHD is tax-efficient and can be held in any account because 100% of its distributions are qualified dividends. In contrast, covered call ETFs like JEPI and JEPQ are best held in tax-deferred or Roth IRAs, as most of their yield is classified as ordinary income and is taxed at higher rates in a taxable brokerage. The video highlights that ETFs like SPYI and QQQI, which generate returns of capital (ROC) via Section 1256 contracts, are actually better suited for taxable accounts due to tax deferral, making their tax advantage less useful in a Roth IRA. The key conclusion is that investors should avoid accidental placement by aligning each fund's tax structure with the optimal account type to maximize long-term returns.
