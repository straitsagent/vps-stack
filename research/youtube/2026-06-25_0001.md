The video explains the origin of the word "dollar," tracing it back to coins minted in Saxony named after their mines, such as the Annaberger. Specifically, coins from a new mine at Joachimstal were called *Joachimstaler*, which was later shortened to *taler*. As the coin spread through Leipzig and the Baltic region, the term evolved into *dalder* in Dutch and finally *dollar* in English. By the 16th century, the word "dollar" was already used in English to refer to a coin outside the British system of pounds, shillings, and pence.
